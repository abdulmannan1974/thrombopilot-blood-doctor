# TIMI Risk Score for UA/NSTEMI

**Type**: Scoring Calculator
**Scraped**: 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Estimates mortality for patients with unstable angina (UA) and non-ST elevation myocardial infarction (NSTEMI). Predicts risk of death, MI, or severe recurrent ischemia requiring urgent revascularization at 14 days.

---

## Scoring Criteria

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Age ≥ 65 years | **+1** |
| ≥ 3 risk factors for coronary artery disease (family history, hypertension, hypercholesterolemia, diabetes, active smoker) | **+1** |
| Known CAD (stenosis ≥ 50%) | **+1** |
| ASA use in past 7 days | **+1** |
| Severe angina (≥ 2 episodes within 24 hours) | **+1** |
| ST changes ≥ 0.5 mm | **+1** |
| Positive cardiac marker (troponin or CK-MB) | **+1** |

**Maximum Score**: 7

---

## Score Interpretation — 14-Day Event Rate

| TIMI Score | Risk of Death, MI, or Urgent Revascularization | Risk Level |
|------------|------------------------------------------------|------------|
| 0–1 | 4.7% | Low |
| 2 | 8.3% | Low–Moderate |
| 3 | 13.2% | Moderate |
| 4 | 19.9% | Moderate–High |
| 5 | 26.2% | High |
| 6–7 | 40.9% | Very High |

---

## Clinical Application

### Low Risk (0–2):
- Conservative strategy may be appropriate
- Medical management with serial troponins
- Non-invasive stress testing before discharge

### Moderate Risk (3–4):
- Consider early invasive strategy (coronary angiography within 24–72 hours)
- Dual antiplatelet therapy
- Anticoagulation (enoxaparin or UFH)

### High Risk (5–7):
- Early invasive strategy strongly recommended (< 24 hours)
- Aggressive antithrombotic therapy
- GP IIb/IIIa inhibitors may be considered
- ICU/CCU monitoring
