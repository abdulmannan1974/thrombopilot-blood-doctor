# TIMI Risk Score for STEMI

**Source**: Thrombosis Canada — https://thrombosiscanada.ca/tools/?calc=timiSTEMI
**Type**: Scoring Calculator
**Scraped**: 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Estimates 30-day mortality in patients with ST-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI).

---

## Scoring Criteria

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age** | |
| — Age < 65 | 0 |
| — Age 65–74 | **+2** |
| — Age ≥ 75 | **+3** |
| Diabetes mellitus, hypertension, or angina | **+1** |
| Systolic BP < 100 mmHg | **+3** |
| Heart rate > 100 bpm | **+2** |
| Killip Class II–IV | **+2** |
| Weight < 67 kg (147.7 lbs) | **+1** |
| Anterior ST elevation or LBBB | **+1** |
| Time to treatment > 4 hours | **+1** |

**Maximum Score**: 14

## Clinical Application

### Low Risk (0–3):
- Standard STEMI management (primary PCI or thrombolysis)
- Expected favorable outcome
- Standard monitoring

### Moderate Risk (4–5):
- Aggressive reperfusion strategy
- Close hemodynamic monitoring
- Consider early catheterization if thrombolysis given

### High Risk (≥ 6):
- Very high mortality risk
- Intensive care management
- Consider mechanical circulatory support if cardiogenic shock
- Multidisciplinary team involvement

### Killip Classification Reference:
| Class | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| I | No heart failure signs |
| II | S3 gallop, rales in < 50% lung fields, elevated JVP |
| III | Pulmonary edema (rales > 50% lung fields) |
| IV | Cardiogenic shock |
