# ABCD2 Score for TIA

**Type**: Scoring Calculator
**Scraped**: 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Estimates risk of stroke after a transient ischemic attack (TIA), according to patient risk factors. Helps guide urgency of workup and intervention.

---

## Scoring Criteria

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| **A** — Age ≥ 60 | **+1** |
| **B** — Blood pressure ≥ 140/90 mmHg at initial evaluation | **+1** |
| **C** — Clinical features of the TIA: | |
| — Unilateral weakness | **+2** |
| — Speech disturbance without weakness | **+1** |
| — Other symptoms | **0** |
| **D** — Duration of symptoms: | |
| — < 10 minutes | **0** |
| — 10–59 minutes | **+1** |
| — ≥ 60 minutes | **+2** |
| **D2** — Diabetes mellitus | **+1** |

**Maximum Score**: 7

---

## Score Interpretation — 2-Day Stroke Risk

| ABCD2 Score | 2-Day Stroke Risk | 7-Day Stroke Risk | Risk Level |
|-------------|-------------------|--------------------|------------|
| 0–3 | 1.0% | 1.2% | Low |
| 4–5 | 4.1% | 5.9% | Moderate |
| 6–7 | 8.1% | 11.7% | High |

## Clinical Application

### Low Risk (0–3):
- Outpatient workup may be considered if rapid assessment clinic available within 24–48 hours
- Brain imaging, vascular imaging, and cardiac assessment
- Start antiplatelet therapy (ASA) immediately

### Moderate Risk (4–5):
- **Urgent assessment** recommended (within 24 hours)
- Consider hospital admission or ED observation
- Brain imaging (CT/MRI), carotid imaging, ECG, echocardiography
- Dual antiplatelet therapy (ASA + clopidogrel) for 21 days, then single agent

### High Risk (6–7):
- **Emergency admission** recommended
- Immediate brain and vascular imaging
- Dual antiplatelet therapy
- Assess for urgent carotid endarterectomy if significant stenosis (> 50–70%)
- Rule out cardioembolic source

### Important Considerations:
- ABCD2 should NOT be used alone — clinical judgment essential
- High-risk features independent of ABCD2:
  - Crescendo TIA (≥ 2 TIAs within 7 days)
  - AF or other cardioembolic source identified
  - Carotid stenosis ≥ 50%
  - Abnormal DWI on MRI
- ALL TIA patients need urgent investigation regardless of score
