# PERC Rule (Pulmonary Embolism Rule-out Criteria)

**Type**: Scoring Calculator
**Scraped**: 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

The PERC rule can rule out pulmonary embolism if ALL criteria are present AND pre-test probability is ≤ 15% (low clinical suspicion). Designed to avoid unnecessary D-dimer testing.

---

## PERC Criteria (ALL must be met to rule out PE)

| # | Criterion |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | Age < 50 years |
| 2 | Heart rate < 100 bpm |
| 3 | Oxygen saturation on room air > 94% |
| 4 | No prior history of DVT or PE |
| 5 | No recent trauma or surgery (within 4 weeks) |
| 6 | No hemoptysis |
| 7 | No exogenous estrogen use (OCP, HRT) |
| 8 | No clinical signs suggesting DVT (unilateral leg swelling) |

---

## Interpretation

| Result | Interpretation | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **All 8 criteria met** AND pre-test probability ≤ 15% | PE ruled out | No further testing needed (D-dimer NOT required) |
| **Any criterion NOT met** | Cannot rule out PE by PERC | Proceed with D-dimer or CTPA based on Wells' score |

---

## Clinical Application

### When to Apply PERC:
- **ONLY** when gestalt/clinical pre-test probability is LOW (≤ 15%)
- PERC is a "rule-out" tool — it should NOT be used to "rule in" PE
- Designed for emergency department settings

### Clinical Decision Pathway:
1. **Clinician gestalt**: Is PE concern low (≤ 15%)?
   - **Yes** → Apply PERC rule
     - All 8 met → **PE ruled out** (no D-dimer needed)
     - Any not met → Order **D-dimer**
   - **No** (moderate/high concern) → Use **Wells' Score** → D-dimer or CTPA

### Important Cautions:
- PERC should NOT be applied if clinical suspicion is moderate or high
- PERC miss rate is approximately 1–2% (comparable to negative D-dimer)
- The rule was derived and validated in US emergency departments
