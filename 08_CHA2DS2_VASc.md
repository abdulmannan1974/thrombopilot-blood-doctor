# CHA2DS2-VASc Score for Atrial Fibrillation Stroke Risk

**Type**: Scoring Calculator
**Scraped**: 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Calculates stroke risk for patients with atrial fibrillation, possibly better than the CHADS2 score. Provides superior discrimination at the lower end of stroke risk.

---

## Scoring Criteria

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| **C** — Congestive Heart Failure History | **+1** |
| **H** — Hypertension History | **+1** |
| **A2** — Age ≥ 75 | **+2** |
| **D** — Diabetes Mellitus | **+1** |
| **S2** — Stroke/TIA/Thromboembolism History | **+2** |
| **V** — Vascular Disease History (previous MI, peripheral arterial disease, or aortic plaque) | **+1** |
| **A** — Age 65–74 | **+1** |
| **Sc** — Sex Category (Female) | **+1** |

**Maximum Score**: 9

## Clinical Application

| Score | Male | Female | Recommendation |
|-------|------|--------|----------------|
| 0 | 0 | — | No antithrombotic therapy |
| 1 | 1 | 1 (female only) | Consider OAC based on individual assessment |
| ≥ 2 | ≥ 2 | ≥ 2 (with non-sex risk factor) | OAC recommended |

### Important Notes:
- Female sex alone (score of 1 in a woman with no other risk factors) does NOT warrant anticoagulation
- The "1 point" threshold for considering OAC applies to MALE patients with a score of 1 or FEMALE patients with a score of 2 (where 1 point = female sex + 1 additional risk factor)
- DOAC preferred over warfarin for most patients with non-valvular AF
- Warfarin required for: mechanical heart valve, moderate-severe mitral stenosis
