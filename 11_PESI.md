# PESI (Pulmonary Embolism Severity Index)

**Source**: Thrombosis Canada — https://thrombosiscanada.ca/tools/?calc=pesi
**Type**: Scoring Calculator
**Scraped**: 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Predicts 30-day outcome of patients with pulmonary embolism using 11 clinical criteria. Stratifies PE patients into risk classes to guide management decisions (outpatient vs. inpatient, thrombolysis consideration).

---

## Scoring Criteria

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Age | **+age in years** |
| Male sex | **+10** |
| Cancer | **+30** |
| Heart failure | **+10** |
| Chronic lung disease | **+10** |
| Heart rate ≥ 110 bpm | **+20** |
| Systolic BP < 100 mmHg | **+30** |
| Respiratory rate ≥ 30/min | **+20** |
| Temperature < 36°C (96.8°F) | **+20** |
| Altered mental status (disorientation, lethargy, stupor, or coma) | **+60** |
| SpO2 < 90% on room air | **+20** |

---

## Risk Classes and 30-Day Mortality

| PESI Class | Score | 30-Day Mortality | Risk Level |
|-----------|-------|------------------|------------|
| **Class I** | ≤ 65 | 0–1.6% | Very Low |
| **Class II** | 66–85 | 1.7–3.5% | Low |
| **Class III** | 86–105 | 3.2–7.1% | Intermediate |
| **Class IV** | 106–125 | 4.0–11.4% | High |
| **Class V** | > 125 | 10.0–24.5% | Very High |

---

## Clinical Application

### Low Risk (Class I–II):
- Consider **outpatient management** or **early discharge** (after initial monitoring)
- Anticoagulation with DOAC monotherapy
- Ensure adequate follow-up

### Intermediate Risk (Class III):
- **Hospital admission** recommended
- Standard anticoagulation
- Monitor for deterioration
- Consider echocardiography and troponin for further risk substratification

### High Risk (Class IV–V):
- **Hospital admission** — consider ICU/HDU
- Assess for hemodynamic instability
- Consider **thrombolysis** if massive PE with hemodynamic compromise
- Advanced imaging and monitoring
