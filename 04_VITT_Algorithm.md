# VITT (Vaccine-Induced Immune Thrombotic Thrombocytopenia) Algorithm

**Source**: Thrombosis Canada — https://thrombosiscanada.ca/tools/?calc=VITT
**Type**: Interactive Algorithm
**Scraped**: 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Interactive algorithm for diagnosing, ruling out, and managing Vaccine-Induced Immune Thrombotic Thrombocytopenia (VITT), also known as Vaccine-Induced Prothrombotic Immune Thrombocytopenia (VIPIT) or Thrombosis with Thrombocytopenia Syndrome (TTS).

---

## Step 1: Symptom Assessment

Does the patient exhibit one or more of the following symptoms?

- Severe persistent headache
- Seizures
- Blurred vision
- Confusion or decreased consciousness
- Abdominal pain
- Nausea/vomiting
- Back pain
- Leg pain or swelling
- Shortness of breath or chest pain
- Petechiae, easy bruising, or bleeding

---

## Step 2: Vaccination Timing

Has the patient received their COVID-19 vaccine (adenoviral vector — AstraZeneca/COVISHIELD or Janssen/Johnson & Johnson) between **4 and 28 days ago**?

- **Yes** → proceed to Step 3
- **No** → VITT unlikely; consider other diagnoses

---

## Step 3: Platelet Count

Does the patient's complete blood count (CBC) show a **platelet count < 150 × 10⁹/L**?

- **Yes** → proceed to Step 4
- **No** → Draw a complete blood count (CBC) and/or send the patient to the nearest emergency department

---

## Step 4: Additional Testing

Perform additional tests with the patient:

| Test | Expected Finding in VITT |
|------|-------------------------|
| **D-dimer** | Markedly elevated (often > 4× ULN) |
| **Fibrinogen** | Low or rapidly declining |
| **Blood film** | Normal (apart from low platelets) — no schistocytes |
| **Imaging** | Based on clinical suspicion — confirms venous or arterial thrombosis |

**Important**: Not all cases of VITT initially present with a clot. A confirmed blood clot is NOT required to make a presumptive diagnosis of VITT.

---

## Diagnostic Criteria

### Definite VITT (all 5 criteria):
1. COVID-19 vaccination (adenoviral vector) 4–28 days prior
2. Thrombosis (venous or arterial, often unusual sites)
3. Thrombocytopenia (platelets < 150 × 10⁹/L)
4. Elevated D-dimer (markedly)
5. Positive anti-PF4 antibodies (ELISA preferred)

### Probable VITT:
- Meets criteria 1–4 but anti-PF4 testing pending or unavailable

### VITT Unlikely:
- Does not meet the above criteria
- **Do NOT proceed to HIT testing** if VITT is ruled out based on clinical presentation

---

## Management of VITT

### DO:
- **Anticoagulate** with non-heparin anticoagulants:
  - Argatroban (IV)
  - Fondaparinux (SC)
  - DOACs (when stable)
- **IV Immunoglobulin (IVIG)**: 1 g/kg daily × 2 days
- **Consult hematology urgently**
- **Send anti-PF4 ELISA** (not rapid HIT assay)
- **Avoid platelet transfusions** (may worsen thrombosis)

### DO NOT:
- **Do NOT give heparin** (UFH or LMWH)
- **Do NOT transfuse platelets** unless life-threatening bleeding
- **Do NOT delay treatment** while awaiting anti-PF4 results
