# HAS-BLED Score for Major Bleeding Risk

**Source**: Thrombosis Canada — https://thrombosiscanada.ca/tools/?calc=hasBled
**Type**: Scoring Calculator
**Scraped**: 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Estimates risk of major bleeding for patients on anticoagulation, to help determine risk–benefit in atrial fibrillation care.

---

## Scoring Criteria

| Letter | Criterion | Points |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **H** | Hypertension History (uncontrolled, systolic BP > 160 mmHg) | **+1** |
| **A** | Abnormal Renal function (dialysis, transplant, Cr > 2.6 mg/dL or > 200 µmol/L) | **+1** |
| **A** | Abnormal Liver function (cirrhosis, bilirubin > 2× normal, AST/ALT/AP > 3× normal) | **+1** |
| **S** | Stroke History | **+1** |
| **B** | Prior Major Bleeding or predisposition to bleeding | **+1** |
| **L** | Labile INR (unstable/high INRs, time in therapeutic range < 60%) | **+1** |
| **E** | Elderly (age ≥ 65) | **+1** |
| **D** | Drugs predisposing to bleeding (antiplatelet agents, NSAIDs) | **+1** |
| **D** | Alcohol use (≥ 8 drinks/week) | **+1** |

**Maximum Score**: 9

---

## Score Interpretation

| HAS-BLED Score | Bleeds per 100 Patient-Years | Risk Category |
|----------------|------------------------------|---------------|
| 0 | 1.13 | Low |
| 1 | 1.02 | Low |
| 2 | 1.88 | Moderate |
| 3 | 3.74 | High |
| 4 | 8.70 | Very High |
| ≥ 5 | 12.50 | Very High |

---

## Clinical Application

### Key Principles:
- **HAS-BLED ≥ 3** = HIGH bleeding risk → warrants caution, NOT a contraindication to anticoagulation
- A high HAS-BLED score should prompt:
  1. **Review and address modifiable risk factors** (uncontrolled BP, labile INR, concurrent antiplatelet/NSAID use, excess alcohol)
  2. **More frequent monitoring**
  3. **Consider DOAC over warfarin** (lower intracranial bleeding risk)
- **Do NOT use HAS-BLED to withhold anticoagulation** in patients who clearly benefit from stroke prevention
- Bleeding risk and stroke risk often increase in parallel

### Modifiable Risk Factors:
- Uncontrolled hypertension → optimize BP control
- Labile INR → switch to DOAC or improve warfarin management
- Concurrent antiplatelet/NSAID → discontinue if not essential
- Excess alcohol → counsel on reduction
