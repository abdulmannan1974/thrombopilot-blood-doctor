# Wells' Criteria for Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)

**Type**: Scoring Calculator
**Scraped**: 2026-03-12

---

## Overview

Calculates Wells' Score reflecting the risk of deep vein thrombosis (DVT). Used to determine pre-test probability and guide diagnostic workup.

---

## Scoring Criteria

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Active cancer (treatment within 6 months, or palliative) | **+1** |
| Bedridden recently > 3 days or major surgery within 4 weeks | **+1** |
| Calf swelling > 3 cm compared to the other leg (measured 10 cm below tibial tuberosity) | **+1** |
| Collateral (nonvaricose) superficial veins present | **+1** |
| Entire leg swollen | **+1** |
| Localized tenderness along the deep venous system | **+1** |
| Pitting edema, greater in the symptomatic leg | **+1** |
| Paralysis, paresis, or recent plaster immobilization of the lower extremity | **+1** |
| Previously documented DVT | **+1** |
| Alternative diagnosis to DVT as likely or more likely | **−2** |

---

## Score Interpretation

### Traditional (Three-Tier) Model

| Score | Risk Category | DVT Prevalence |
|-------|--------------|----------------|
| ≤ 0 | Low | ~5% |
| 1–2 | Moderate | ~17% |
| ≥ 3 | High | ~53% |

### Simplified (Two-Tier) Model

| Score | Risk Category | Approach |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| ≤ 1 | DVT Unlikely | D-dimer testing |
| ≥ 2 | DVT Likely | Compression ultrasonography (CUS) |

---

## Clinical Application

### DVT Unlikely (Score ≤ 1):
1. Order **D-dimer** (high-sensitivity assay)
2. If D-dimer **negative** → DVT excluded
3. If D-dimer **positive** → Proceed to **compression ultrasonography (CUS)**
4. Consider age-adjusted D-dimer cutoff (Age × 10 µg/L for patients > 50 years)

### DVT Likely (Score ≥ 2):
1. Proceed directly to **compression ultrasonography (CUS)**
2. If CUS **positive** → Diagnose DVT, start anticoagulation
3. If CUS **negative** → Repeat CUS in 5–7 days OR order D-dimer
4. If CUS negative AND D-dimer negative → DVT excluded
